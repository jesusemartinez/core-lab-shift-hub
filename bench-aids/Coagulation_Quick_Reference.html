✓ Pathway

Intrinsic + Extrinsic → Common pathway → Fibrin clot

The Coagulation Cascade
Intrinsic + Extrinsic → Common → Fibrin
Intrinsic (contact activation): XII → XI → IX + VIII → X. Extrinsic (tissue factor): TF + VIIa → X. Both converge on the common pathway: X + Va → Prothrombin (II) → Thrombin → Fibrinogen (I) → Fibrin. Factor XIII cross-links fibrin for stability.
Primary Hemostasis
Platelet plug formation — fast, temporary
Vessel injury → vWF released → platelets adhere (GPIb-vWF) → activated (shape change, granule release) → aggregate (GPIIb/IIIa-fibrinogen bridge). Forms the primary platelet plug within seconds. Followed by secondary hemostasis (coagulation cascade).
Secondary Hemostasis
Fibrin mesh stabilizes platelet plug
Thrombin converts fibrinogen → fibrin monomers → polymerize → cross-linked by Factor XIII. The fibrin mesh traps RBCs and platelets, forming a stable clot. This is what PT, aPTT, and fibrinogen assess.
Tests

What each test measures and which factors it evaluates

PT / INR
Prothrombin Time — Extrinsic + Common pathway
Measures extrinsic pathway (VII) + common (X, V, II, I). Normal PT: 11–13 sec. INR: normalized ratio (target 2–3 for therapeutic anticoagulation). Elevated by: warfarin, liver disease, vitamin K deficiency, Factor VII deficiency, DIC.
Factors: VII, X, V, II, I Warfarin · Liver · Vit K deficiency
aPTT
Activated Partial Thromboplastin Time — Intrinsic + Common
Measures intrinsic pathway (XII, XI, IX, VIII) + common (X, V, II, I). Normal aPTT: 25–35 sec. Elevated by: heparin (UFH), hemophilia A (VIII↓), hemophilia B (IX↓), vWD (severe), lupus anticoagulant, DIC. Not affected by warfarin.
Factors: XII, XI, IX, VIII, X, V, II, I Heparin · Hemophilia A/B · LA
Fibrinogen (Factor I)
Acute phase reactant; substrate for thrombin
Converted to fibrin by thrombin. Normal: 200–400 mg/dL. ↓ Fibrinogen: DIC (consumed), severe liver disease, thrombolytic therapy, dysfibrinogenemia. ↑ Fibrinogen: Acute phase reaction (infection, inflammation, pregnancy) — may not reflect true synthesis.
↓ DIC · Liver failure · Thrombolytics ↑ Acute phase reaction
D-Dimer
Fibrin degradation product — clot formation AND breakdown
Released when cross-linked fibrin is degraded by plasmin. Normal: <0.5 µg/mL FEU (institution-specific). ↑ D-Dimer: PE/DVT, DIC, MI, surgery, trauma, infection, pregnancy, liver disease, malignancy. High sensitivity, low specificity — useful for ruling OUT VTE, not confirming it.
DIC · PE/DVT · Post-op · Sepsis Negative → rules out VTE (high NPV)
Mixing Studies
Patient plasma + Normal plasma (1:1) → Immediate + Incubated
Used when PT or aPTT is prolonged to differentiate factor deficiency from inhibitor. Mix patient plasma 1:1 with normal pooled plasma. Corrects to normal: factor deficiency (normal plasma supplies missing factor). Does NOT correct: inhibitor present (LA, specific factor inhibitor, heparin).
Corrects → Factor deficiency No correction → Inhibitor
Thrombin Time & Reptilase Time
Final common step — fibrinogen → fibrin
TT tests only the final conversion step. Elevated by heparin, low fibrinogen, dysfibrinogenemia. Reptilase (snake venom) bypasses heparin — if TT elevated but reptilase normal → heparin effect.
