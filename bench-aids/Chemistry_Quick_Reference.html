🟣 Extended Liver Panel
AnalyteReference Range↓ Decreased↑ IncreasedClinical Significance
GGTU/LM: 8 -- 61
F: 5 -- 36		Rarely significantAlcohol use, cholestasis, drug induction (phenytoin, barbiturates), pancreatic diseaseConfirms hepatobiliary source of ↑ALP
Sensitive marker of alcohol use
Direct Bilirubinmg/dL0.0 -- 0.3N/ABiliary obstruction, Dubin-Johnson, Rotor, hepatocellular diseaseConjugated; water-soluble; excreted in urine
Liver Injury Patterns:
Hepatocellular: ↑↑AST/ALT (>10x), mild ↑ALP/bilirubin -- viral hepatitis, drug toxicity (APAP), ischemic hepatitis
Cholestatic: ↑↑ALP/GGT (>3x), moderate ↑bilirubin, mild ↑AST/ALT -- biliary obstruction, PBC, PSC, drug-induced
Infiltrative: ↑ALP out of proportion, mild transaminase ↑ -- granulomatous disease, metastatic cancer, amyloidosis
Alcoholic: AST:ALT ratio >2:1, AST rarely >300, ↑GGT -- classic for alcoholic hepatitis

PatternKey FindingsDiagnosis / Workup
Prerenal Azotemia↑BUN, ↑Cr, BUN/Cr >20:1Dehydration, CHF, shock, GI bleed
Intrinsic Renal Failure↑BUN, ↑Cr, BUN/Cr 10-15:1, ↑K, ↓Ca, ↑PhosATN, glomerulonephritis, interstitial nephritis

Tumor Lysis↑K, ↑Phos, ↑Uric acid, ↑LDH, ↓CaPost-chemo emergency; hydration, rasburicase
Hepatocellular Injury↑↑AST/ALT (>10x), mild ↑ALP, ↑BilirubinViral hepatitis, drug toxicity, ischemia
Cholestatic Pattern↑↑ALP, ↑GGT, ↑Direct bilirubinBiliary obstruction, PBC, PSC
