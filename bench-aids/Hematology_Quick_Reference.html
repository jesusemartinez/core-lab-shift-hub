RBC

Red Blood Cell Morphology

✓ Normal

Baseline appearances — know these before evaluating abnormals

Normal RBC Appearance
Discocyte — biconcave disc
Round, uniform cells with smooth edges and a clear central pallor occupying approximately 1/3 of the cell diameter. All cells should be roughly the same size and color.
Size: 7–8 µm Pallor: ~1/3 diameter Color: pink-salmon
No reportable findings → Report: RBCMNO
Normocytic RBCs
Normal size — approximately the size of a small lymphocyte nucleus
RBCs should be roughly the same diameter as a small lymphocyte nucleus (useful size reference on every smear). MCV 80–100 fL. Consistent sizing with low RDW.
MCV: 80–100 fL RDW: <14.5% ~lymph nucleus size
Microcytic / Hypochromic
Small pale cells — iron deficiency pattern
Smaller than a lymphocyte nucleus. Central pallor exceeds 1/3 of diameter (hypochromia). Classic for iron deficiency anemia, thalassemia trait, and anemia of chronic disease.
MCV: <80 fL MCH: <27 pg Pallor: >1/3
Macrocytic RBCs
Large cells — oval macrocytes common in megaloblastic
Larger than a lymphocyte nucleus. Often oval-shaped (oval macrocytes) in B12/folate deficiency. Round macrocytes in liver disease, alcohol, hypothyroidism, MDS. Less central pallor.
MCV: >100 fL Oval shape → B12/folate Round shape → liver/EtOH
✕ Abnormal

Reportable RBC findings — follow decision flow below

Workflow → Rouleaux?› Agglutination?› Shift/Poikilocytosis?› Inclusions?› NRBC?› Polychromasia?› Report RBCMNO
Anisocytosis
Variation in RBC size — elevated RDW
Mixture of cell sizes (small, normal, large) on the same smear. Correlates with elevated RDW on CBC. Seen in iron deficiency, B12/folate deficiency, mixed deficiencies, transfused patients.
Grade and report based on degree of variation
Poikilocytosis
Variation in RBC shape — general term
Mixture of abnormal shapes (teardrops, elliptocytes, target cells, etc.) on the same smear. Non-specific — report the specific abnormal shapes found rather than just "poikilocytosis."
Report specific shapes; note if shift present
Rouleaux
Linear coin-stacking — elevated plasma proteins
RBCs stacked in linear columns in the thin zone. Multiple myeloma, Waldenström's, infection, inflammation. Disperses with saline dilution (vs. agglutination). Only report when seen in the thin reading area.
Remake smear if new finding; confirm thin zone
Report: ROULEAUX PRESENT
Agglutination
Irregular grape-like RBC clusters
True clumping in irregular clusters (not linear stacks). Cold agglutinins, AIHA. Confirm with wet mount before reporting. Falsely lowers RBC count and raises MCV/MCHC.
✓ Confirm with wet mount first
Report: AGGLUTINATION PRESENT
Polychromasia
Blue-gray tinge — residual RNA = reticulocytes
Bluish-gray cells from residual RNA. Represent reticulocytes on supravital stain. Active erythropoiesis, hemolysis, hemorrhage, treatment response.
DI-60: >3/RBC field (16 fields) Manual: >6/100× field
Report: POLYCHROMASIA PRESENT
Nucleated RBCs (NRBC)
Erythroblasts — dark nucleus in RBC precursor
Precursor RBCs with retained nucleus. Normal only in neonates. Adults: severe anemia, BM stress, extramedullary hematopoiesis, leukemia. Correct WBC count if ≥5/100 WBC.
DI-60: >5% → report % & abs Scan only → OCCASIONAL NRBC
Report: SEE NUCLEATED RBC COUNT / NRBC PRESENT / PSNRBC
Target Cells
Codocytes — bull's-eye pattern
Dark center → pale ring → dark outer rim (3-zone). Liver disease, HbC/S/thalassemia, iron deficiency, post-splenectomy. Thin cells with excess membrane.
1–5: SLT 6–15: MOD >15: MKD
Report: TARGET CELLS [grade] per field
Elliptocytes
Ovalocytes — pencil/oval-shaped RBCs
Elongated oval cells. Hereditary elliptocytosis, iron deficiency, megaloblastic anemia, myelofibrosis. Small numbers (<10/field) are normal in healthy individuals.
DI-60: >15/field = MKD Manual: >10/field = MKD
Report: ELLIPTOCYTES [grade] per field
Burr Cells (Echinocytes)
Crenated cells — 10–30 uniform short spicules
Uniformly distributed short projections around the entire cell. Uremia, liver disease, pyruvate kinase deficiency, artifact. Confirm on fresh smear — echinocytes often artifact-induced.
1–5: SLT 6–15: MOD >15: MKD
Report: BURR CELLS [grade] per field
Acanthocytes (Spur Cells)
3–12 irregular, variably-sized thorny projections
Irregular spicules of unequal length and spacing — NOT an artifact (persist on fresh smear). Abetalipoproteinemia, severe liver disease, McLeod phenotype, neuroacanthocytosis.
DI-60: 6–15 = MOD; >15 = MKD Manual: 5–10 = MOD; >10 = MKD
Report: ACANTHOCYTES [grade] per field
Teardrop Cells (Dacrocytes)
Pear-shaped — one elongated end
Classic for myelofibrosis — cells squeezed through fibrotic marrow. Also megaloblastic anemia, thalassemia, infiltrative BM processes, and iron deficiency. Tails should all point in same direction on well-made smear.
DI-60: >15/field = MKD Manual: >10/field = MKD
Report: TEARDROPS [grade] per field
Spherocytes
Dense, small, round — absent central pallor
Small densely staining cells with complete loss of central pallor. Hereditary spherocytosis, AIHA, transfusion reactions, burns, clostridial sepsis. Elevated MCHC is a key lab correlate.
DI-60: 1–5/field = SLT Manual: 1–4/field = SLT
Report: SPHEROCYTES [grade] per field
Fragmented RBCs (Schistocytes)
Helmet cells, triangles, microspherocytes
RBC fragments from mechanical shearing. TTP/HUS, DIC, MAHA, prosthetic heart valves, HELLP. ⚠ Often an emergency — notify clinician immediately.
DI-60: 6–15 = MOD; >15 = MKD Manual: 5–10 = MOD; >10 = MKD
Report: FRAGMENTED RBCs [grade] per field
Sickle Cells (Drepanocytes)
Elongated crescent — rigid polymerized HbS
Crescent or sickle-shaped RBCs from HbS polymerization. Sickle cell disease (HbSS, HbSC, HbS-thal). Often accompanied by target cells, Howell-Jolly bodies, NRBCs, polychromasia.
Report if ANY are seen
Report: SICKLE CELLS PRESENT
RBC Inclusions
Basophilic Stippling (BS) · Howell-Jolly Bodies (HJ) · Pappenheimer Bodies (PP)
Basophilic Stippling
Punctate blue dots — aggregated ribosomes
Lead poisoning, thalassemia, sideroblastic anemia, pyrimidine-5′-nucleotidase deficiency. Coarse stippling is more pathologic than fine.
DI-60: >1/RBC fieldManual: ≥1 per 3 fields
BASOPHILIC STIPPLING PRESENT
Howell-Jolly Bodies
Single dark round nuclear remnant
DNA remnants normally removed by the spleen. Asplenia, hyposplenia, megaloblastic anemia, hemolytic anemia. Usually one per cell.
DI-60: >1/RBC fieldManual: ≥1 per 3 fields
HOWELL-JOLLY BODIES PRESENT
Pappenheimer Bodies
Multiple irregular iron-containing granule clusters
Iron-containing granules in clusters — multiple per cell (distinguishes from HJ). Sideroblastic anemia, post-splenectomy, hemolytic anemia.
DI-60: >1/RBC fieldManual: ≥1 per 3 fields
PAPPENHEIMER BODIES PRESENT
Dimorphic Population
Two distinct RBC populations — confirm histogram + smear
Two peaks on RBC histogram AND two visually distinct populations on smear (e.g., normochromic + microcytic/hypochromic). Iron deficiency with transfusion, sideroblastic anemia, partially treated nutritional deficiencies.
Requires 2 peaks on histogram AND 2 populations confirmed on smear
Report: DIMORPHIC POPULATION PRESENT

Neutrophil (Segmented Poly)
Seg / PMN — most common WBC (50–70%)
Pale pink-tan cytoplasm with fine lilac granules. Nucleus has 2–4 lobes connected by thin filaments. Lobes become darker and more condensed than the cytoplasm.
50–70% 1.8–7.7 ×10³/µL 2–4 lobes
Lymphocyte
Small lymphocyte — 20–40%; nucleus reference for RBC sizing
Large, round, deeply staining nucleus filling most of the cell. Scant rim of pale blue cytoplasm. Small lymphocyte nucleus ≈ normal RBC diameter — use as sizing reference on every smear.
20–40% 1.0–4.8 ×10³/µL Size ref for RBCs
Monocyte
Largest normal WBC — 2–8%; kidney/folded nucleus
Largest normal peripheral blood cell. Kidney, horseshoe, or folded nucleus with lacy/open chromatin. Gray-blue cytoplasm with fine vacuoles and sometimes pink granules. Irregular cell border.
2–8% 0.2–0.8 ×10³/µL 14–20 µm
Eosinophil
Bilobed nucleus; large bright orange-red granules; 1–4%
Bilobed nucleus (2 lobes connected by thin filament). Cytoplasm packed with large, uniform, bright orange-red granules that fill the entire cytoplasm. Easy to spot due to vivid color. Elevated in allergies, parasites, eosinophilic disorders.
1–4% 0.0–0.5 ×10³/µL Bright orange granules
Basophil
Large dark purple granules obscuring nucleus; <1%
Rarest normal WBC. Contains large, coarse, dark purple/black granules that often obscure the bilobed nucleus. Granules may wash out in thin areas leaving an empty-looking cell. Elevated in CML, allergic reactions, myeloproliferative disorders.
<1% 0.0–0.1 ×10³/µL Granules obscure nucleus

Toxic Granulation
Dark purple primary granules in neutrophils
Abnormally prominent, dark purple/black primary azurophilic granules due to accelerated myelopoiesis — granules retain their staining rather than maturing to secondary (specific) granules. Infection, sepsis, severe inflammation, G-CSF therapy, pregnancy.
Seen in ≥1 of every 5 neutrophils
Report: TOXIC GRANULATION PRESENT
Döhle Bodies
Pale blue oval cytoplasmic inclusion (residual RER)
Pale blue, oval or elongated inclusions in neutrophil cytoplasm representing aggregated rough endoplasmic reticulum (ribosomes). Often co-occur with toxic granulation. Same clinical associations: infection, sepsis, inflammation, pregnancy, G-CSF, May-Hegglin anomaly.
Seen in ≥1 of every 5 neutrophils
Report: DÖHLE BODIES PRESENT
Smudge Cells
Basket cells — ruptured fragile lymphocytes
Ruptured lymphocyte nuclei smeared across the slide during preparation. Fragile lymphocytes with reduced cytoskeletal proteins. Classic for CLL. Perform differential on albumin-coated slide; WBC estimate on regular slide.
Make albumin prep for diff WBC est on regular slide Order TECHRV
Report: SMRPAT + Order TECHRV
Reactive Lymphocytes
Atypical / Downey cells — large, abundant basophilic cytoplasm
Large lymphocytes with abundant, deeply basophilic cytoplasm that characteristically "molds" or indents around adjacent RBCs. Activated T-cells. Viral infections: EBV/mono, CMV, hepatitis, HIV, COVID-19.
Abs Lymph >5,000 AND ≥20% of lymphs are reactive
Report: REACTIVE LYMPHOCYTES PRESENT
Neoplastic Lymphocytes
Monotonous abnormal lymphoid cells — check history first
Abnormal lymphocytes suggesting lymphoproliferative disorder — monotonous population, irregular nuclei, cleaved cells, prolymphocytes, or hairy projections. Always check prior history before reporting.
Check prior history first ≥10% of WBC
Report: NEOPLASTIC LYMPHS PRESENT
Hypersegmented Neutrophils
≥5 lobes — right shift
Neutrophils with ≥5 lobes (normal = 2–4). Classic for megaloblastic anemia (B12/folate deficiency), MDS, renal failure, hereditary hypersegmentation. A single 6-lobed cell is significant. Count lobes carefully.
≥2 cells with 6 lobes AND majority of others 4+ lobes
Report: HYPERSEGMENTED POLYS PRESENT
Hyposegmented Neutrophils
Pelger-Huët — pince-nez bilobed; left shift
≤2 lobes with condensed "peanut" or dumbbell chromatin. Pseudo-Pelger-Huët in MDS (acquired); hereditary form is benign. Distinguish from bands — chromatin is condensed/dark, not immature/pale. Also with some drugs.
Any significant number — report
Report: HYPOSEGMENTED POLYS PRESENT
Increased Bands
Stabs — horseshoe/U-shaped unsegmented nucleus
Immature neutrophils with U-shaped nucleus — no lobulation or filaments. Nucleus is parallel-sided throughout its width. Left shift indicating acute bacterial infection, stress, BM stimulation. Normal ≤5%; report if >10%.
>10% of WBC
Report: INCREASED BANDS PRESENT

Platelet Clumps
EDTA-induced aggregation — pseudothrombocytopenia
Platelets clumped together — often EDTA-dependent in vitro artifact. Causes falsely low automated PLT count. Consider collecting in citrate or heparin tube. May trigger critical PLT flag. Run PLT-F channel to confirm.
Run PLT-F channel Perform PLT estimate
Append CLMPT2. Report XN PLT with ">" if below ref range.

Platelet Satelliting
Platelets rosetting around neutrophils
Platelets adhere in a ring around neutrophils (occasionally monocytes). EDTA-dependent phenomenon. Causes falsely low PLT count. WBC count may also be affected as neutrophils may not be counted correctly.
Append free text note to PLT count
Append: "PLATELETS ARE SATELLITING, MAY AFFECT PLATELET COUNT. PLATELET COUNT MAY BE HIGHER."
Report: SPLT
Large / Bizarre Platelets
Giant platelets ≥ RBC size; abnormal shapes
Large platelets: Approach or exceed RBC size — ITP, MYH9-related disorders, Bernard-Soulier, reactive thrombocytosis, MPN. Bizarre platelets: Irregular shapes, abnormal granulation — MDS, MPN. Perform PLT estimate; run PLT-F if flagged.
Perform PLT estimate Run PLT-F if flag present
Report: SPLT
